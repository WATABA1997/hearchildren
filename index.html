About Us

HearChildren Orphanage

Hear Children is a community‑founded orphanage located in the rural village of Malube in eastern Uganda.
The organization was established in response to the growing number of vulnerable children in the area many of whom had lost parents to illness, poverty‑related hardships, or displacement.
With limited formal social services available in the region, local leaders, caregivers, and compassionate residents came together to create a safe haven where children could find stability, care, and hope.

Since its founding, the orphanage has provided shelter, nutritious meals, basic healthcare, and emotional support to children who would otherwise be left without a dependable support system. Beyond meeting immediate needs, Hear Children emphasizes education as a pathway out of poverty. The organization works closely with local schools and community volunteers to ensure that each child receives consistent learning opportunities and mentorship.

Our Mission.

Three main objectives underpin the work of HearChildren Orphanage: Provide shelter and basic needs,
Education and Health Care, Life Skills Development

1. Provide Shelter and Basic Needs

HCO aims to offer a safe and nurturing environment for children without parental care.
This includes providing adequate shelter, food, clothing, and other essential amenities
to ensure a comfortable living situation.

2. Education and Health Care

Provide quality education in order to gain knowledge and skills necessary for their future.

To provide access to medical care, regular health check-ups, and psychological support to promote a healthy lifestyle

3. Life Skills Development

We focus on equipping children with essential life skills, such as financial literacy, communication, and problem-solving,
to help them become self-sufficient and confident individuals.
